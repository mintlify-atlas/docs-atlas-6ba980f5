## What is Cribado de Desarrollo Infantil?

Cribado de Desarrollo Infantil is a web-based clinical screening platform that administers the **AVDB (Actividades de la Vida Diaria Básicas)** assessment to children between 3 and 12 years old. It allows clinicians, researchers, and educators to quickly identify children at risk for difficulties in everyday self-care and adaptive functioning — without requiring direct observation by a specialist.

The platform collects responses from a caregiver or clinician, calculates a risk profile across four functional domains, and stores results anonymously in a structured database for longitudinal analysis and population-level research.

<Note>
  All data is collected without personally identifiable information. No names, addresses, or contact details are stored. See [How data is collected](#how-data-is-collected) below.
</Note>

## Clinical purpose

Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) are foundational to a child's independence and school readiness. Difficulties in ADLs are early indicators of neurodevelopmental conditions such as Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), and Intellectual Disability — and can persist without targeted intervention.

The AVDB assessment is designed to:

- Detect functional difficulties in children before formal diagnosis
- Monitor developmental progress over time
- Generate structured data for clinical and academic research
- Support educators and therapists in identifying children who need additional support

## Assessment dimensions

The AVDB questionnaire measures four domains of basic daily functioning. Each domain is scored independently and contributes to an overall risk classification.

| Phase | Domain | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Alimentación** (Feeding) | Eating behaviors, food acceptance, mealtime independence |
| 2 | **Higiene Personal** (Personal Hygiene) | Bathing, hand washing, dental care, toileting |
| 3 | **Vestido** (Dressing) | Dressing and undressing, fastening buttons and zippers |
| 4 | **Funcionamiento Diario** (Daily Functioning) | General adaptive behavior across home and school routines |

## Scoring methodology

Each question is answered on a 4-point frequency scale by the caregiver or clinician:

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 3 | Siempre | Performs independently and habitually |
| 2 | A veces | Inconsistent; needs help or makes frequent errors |
| 1 | Nunca | Not performed or not yet acquired |
| 0 | No sabe / No oportunidad | Unknown or no opportunity to observe |

Some items are **inverted questions** — typically sensory sensitivity items where doing something habitually is a negative indicator. For these items, the score is flipped (3→1, 1→3) before aggregation.

The total and per-phase scores are expressed as a percentage of the maximum possible score, then classified into one of four risk bands:

| Range | Color | Classification |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| ≥ 75% | Green | Optimal Development |
| 50–74% | Yellow | Emerging / Monitoring |
| 25–49% | Orange | Moderate Difficulty |
| < 25% | Red | Significant Risk |

## How data is collected

All submissions are stored anonymously in Supabase. The platform collects:

- **Birth year** (not full date of birth)
- **Gender**
- **Neurodevelopmental conditions** (self-reported from a predefined list)
- **Answer scores** per phase and aggregated total
- **Test type** (`preescolar` or `escolar`)
- **Source** (`web` for online submissions, `manual` for clinician-entered data)

No names, email addresses, or other identifying information are collected or stored at any point.

The conditions collected during onboarding are:

- Desarrollo típico
- Trastorno del espectro del autismo
- Trastorno por déficit de atención e hiperactividad
- Discapacidad intelectual
- Altas capacidades
- Otros (free text)
- Prefiero no contestar
