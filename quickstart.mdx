This guide walks through a complete screening session on the Cribado de Desarrollo Infantil platform. Follow each step in order — the platform enforces a linear flow from home screen through to results.

<Note>
  All data collected during the assessment is stored anonymously. No names or personally identifiable information are required at any point. See [Introduction — how data is collected](/introduction#how-data-is-collected).
</Note>

## Complete a screening session

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the platform">
    Navigate to the platform home screen at `/`. You will see two large selection cards — one for each age track.

    The home screen does not require authentication. Any caregiver, clinician, or educator can start an assessment without an account.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the child's age group">
    Choose the track that matches the child's current age:

    | Track | Age range | Route |
    |-------|-----------|-------|
    | **Preescolar** | 3–6 years | `/presentation?type=preescolar` |
    | **Escolar** | 7–12 years | `/presentation?type=escolar` |

    Selecting the wrong track will produce scores calibrated to the wrong developmental expectations. If you are unsure, use the child's age in years at the time of assessment.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Read the test presentation">
    The presentation screen (`/presentation`) explains the purpose of the assessment and what to expect. Read through it before proceeding.

    The selected track (`preescolar` or `escolar`) is carried as a URL query parameter through the entire session. Do not navigate away or refresh the browser mid-session, as this will reset the query state.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the onboarding form">
    The onboarding screen (`/onboarding`) collects demographic information about the child. All fields are required.

    **Age** — enter the child's current age in years. The platform validates that the age falls within the range for the selected test track (3–6 for Preescolar, 7–12 for Escolar). Birth year is calculated automatically from the age you enter.

    **Gender** — select Niño, Niña, or Otro.

    **Neurodevelopmental conditions** — select all that apply from the list below. You may select multiple.

    - Desarrollo típico
    - Trastorno del espectro del autismo
    - Trastorno por déficit de atención e hiperactividad
    - Discapacidad intelectual
    - Altas capacidades
    - Otros (free text field)
    - Prefiero no contestar

    <Info>
      If a formal diagnosis has not been made yet, select **Desarrollo típico** or **Prefiero no contestar**. This field informs research segmentation, not the scoring algorithm.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Accept the informed consent disclaimer">
    The disclaimer screen (`/disclaimer`) presents the full informed consent statement for this research study, conducted by the Universidad de Extremadura. You must actively choose one of two options:

    - **Sí, acepto participar** — accepts consent and proceeds to the questionnaire
    - **No, no acepto participar** — declines and returns you to the home screen

    No data is stored until a response is fully submitted at the end of the assessment.

    <Warning>
      If the caregiver or guardian does not consent, select "No, no acepto participar". You will be returned to the home screen and no data will be collected.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete each assessment phase">
    The test screen (`/test`) presents the four AVDB phases in sequence. Each phase must be completed before the next one unlocks.

    | Phase | Domain |
    |-------|--------|
    | 1 | Alimentación (Feeding) |
    | 2 | Higiene Personal (Personal Hygiene) |
    | 3 | Vestido (Dressing) |
    | 4 | Funcionamiento Diario (Daily Functioning) |

    For each question, select the frequency that best describes the child's typical behavior:

    | Option | Score | Meaning |
    |--------|-------|---------|
    | **Siempre** | 3 | Performs independently and habitually |
    | **A veces** | 2 | Inconsistent; needs help or makes frequent errors |
    | **Nunca** | 1 | Not performed or skill not yet acquired |
    | **No sabe / No oportunidad** | 0 | Unknown or no opportunity to observe |

    Answer based on the child's **typical**, day-to-day behavior — not their best or worst performance.

    <Tip>
      Some questions are **inverted items** — these are sensory sensitivity questions where answering "Siempre" (always) indicates a concern, not a strength. The platform handles score inversion automatically; you do not need to adjust your answers. Simply respond with the frequency that accurately reflects what you observe.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit and view results">
    After completing all four phases, submit the assessment. The platform will:

    1. Calculate per-phase scores as a percentage of the maximum possible score
    2. Aggregate an overall total score
    3. Classify each score into a risk band
    4. Store the result anonymously in Supabase
    5. Redirect to the results screen (`/success`)

    The results screen displays color-coded risk classifications for each domain and the overall score:

    | Color | Range | Classification |
    |-------|-------|----------------|
    | Green | ≥ 75% | Optimal Development |
    | Yellow | 50–74% | Emerging / Monitoring |
    | Orange | 25–49% | Moderate Difficulty |
    | Red | < 25% | Significant Risk |

    Results in the **Orange** or **Red** bands warrant referral to a pediatric occupational therapist or developmental specialist for full evaluation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Accessing the admin dashboard

Aggregate results and cohort analytics are available at `/admin`. This route requires authentication via `/login`.

The dashboard is intended for clinicians and researchers managing multiple assessments. It is not part of the standard screening workflow.
