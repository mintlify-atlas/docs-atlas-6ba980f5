This page explains how individual responses are converted into phase scores, how phase scores combine into a total, and how to interpret the colour-coded risk classification.

---

## The four-point Likert scale

Each question is answered using a four-point scale. The value assigned to each option is used directly in score calculations.

| Option | Value | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Siempre** | 3 | Performed habitually and independently |
| **A veces** | 2 | Inconsistent, with assistance, or frequent errors |
| **Nunca** | 1 | Not performed or not yet acquired |
| **No sabe / No oportunidad** | 0 | Not observed or no opportunity has arisen |

Higher values indicate stronger, more independent performance. The sum of all values within a phase produces the phase raw score.

## Inverted questions

Some questions describe behaviours where a high frequency is a concern rather than a strength — for example, sensory hypersensitivity or rigid routines. For these items, the scoring is **inverted** before being added to the phase total.

**Inversion mapping:**

| Response | Standard value | Inverted value |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Siempre | 3 | 1 |
| A veces | 2 | 2 |
| Nunca | 1 | 3 |
| No sabe / No oportunidad | 0 | 0 |

<Info>
  You do not need to adjust your answers for inverted questions. The platform applies the inversion automatically when calculating scores.
</Info>

## Phase scores and total score

Each phase score is the sum of the (possibly inverted) values for all questions in that phase. Phase scores aggregate into a total score.

    | Higiene Personal (Aseo) | 90 |
    | Vestido | 63 |
    | Funcionamiento Diario | 51 |
    | **Total** | **252** |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Escolar">

The results page displays your **total score as a fraction of the maximum** (e.g., 189/252) alongside a per-phase breakdown showing the raw score for each of the four phases.

## Risk classification

Each phase score — and the total score — is converted to a **percentage of its maximum** and assigned to one of four risk levels.

| Level | Colour | Percentage threshold | Label |
|-------|--------|---------------------|-------|
| 4 | Green | ≥ 75% | Fortaleza / Desarrollo Óptimo |
| 3 | Yellow | 50–74% | Desempeño Emergente / En Seguimiento |
| 2 | Orange | 25–49% | Dificultad Moderada / Precaución |
| 1 | Red | < 25% | Riesgo Significativo |

**Example:** A Preescolar child scores 30 out of 48 on Alimentación. That is 62.5%, which falls in the **Yellow** band (Desempeño Emergente / En Seguimiento).

<Warning>
  The risk classification is a screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument. Results indicating Riesgo Significativo or Dificultad Moderada should be followed up with a qualified occupational therapist or paediatric specialist.
</Warning>

## Radar chart in the admin dashboard

The admin dashboard displays a **radar chart** that visualises the child's performance across all factors within each phase. Each axis of the radar corresponds to one scoring factor (e.g., Sensibilidad oral, Destreza manual en alimentación).

- The chart provides a quick visual profile of relative strengths and areas of concern across all domains.
- Factors that score proportionally lower appear closer to the centre of the chart.
- The chart reflects the same percentage-of-maximum calculation used for the risk classification, making it consistent with the colour-coded results.

<Tip>
  Use the radar chart alongside the colour-coded phase scores to identify specific factors driving a lower phase total — for example, a phase in the Yellow band may contain one factor in the Red band and others in the Green band.
</Tip>
