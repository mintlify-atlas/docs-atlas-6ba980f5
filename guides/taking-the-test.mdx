This guide walks you through the full process of completing a developmental screening, from selecting the age group to viewing your results.

<Note>
  All responses are anonymous. No personally identifiable information is collected during the screening. There is no time limit — you can take as long as needed.
</Note>

## Before you begin

You will need:

- The child's age (3–12 years)
- Knowledge of the child's daily routines and behaviours across feeding, hygiene, dressing, and daily functioning
- Information about any neurodevelopmental diagnoses, if applicable

---

## Completing the screening

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select an age group">
    On the landing page, choose the screening track that matches the child's age:

    <Columns cols={2}>
      <Card title="Preescolar" icon="baby">
        For children aged **3–6 years**.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Escolar" icon="graduation-cap">
        For children aged **7–12 years**.
      </Card>
    </Columns>

    The age group determines which set of questions and scoring norms are applied. See [Test types](/guides/test-types) for a full comparison.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the presentation screen">
    A brief introduction explains the purpose of the screening, what the phases cover, and how the results are used. Read this carefully before proceeding.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete the onboarding form">
    Enter the following information about the child:

    - **Age** — enter the child's current age in years. The platform enforces the valid range for the selected track (3–6 for Preescolar, 7–12 for Escolar). Birth year is calculated automatically.
    - **Gender** — select Niño, Niña, or Otro.
    - **Neurodevelopmental profile** — select all that apply:

    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Available neurodevelopmental conditions">
        - Desarrollo típico
        - Trastorno del espectro del autismo
        - Trastorno por déficit de atención e hiperactividad
        - Discapacidad intelectual
        - Altas capacidades
        - Otros (free text field)
        - Prefiero no contestar
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>

    Multiple conditions can be selected. This information contextualises the results but does not affect scoring.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Accept the informed consent">
    The disclaimer screen presents the full informed consent statement for this research study, conducted by the Universidad de Extremadura (investigator: Sabina Barrios Fernández). It explains the study's purpose, data anonymity, and your right to withdraw.

    You must actively choose one of two options:
    - **Sí, acepto participar** — proceeds to the questionnaire
    - **No, no acepto participar** — returns to the home screen; no data is collected

    <Info>
      This study complies with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR/RGPD) and Spanish data protection law (Ley Orgánica 3/2018).
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a phase from the test hub">
    The test hub displays four phase cards. Each phase covers a distinct domain of daily living skills:

    <Columns cols={2}>
      <Card title="Alimentación" icon="utensils">
        Feeding skills, oral sensitivity, and mealtime behaviour.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Higiene Personal" icon="droplets">
        Toileting, dental hygiene, and personal grooming.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Vestido" icon="shirt">
        Dressing and undressing tasks.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Funcionamiento Diario" icon="brain">
        Executive function and daily routine management.
      </Card>
    </Columns>

    Phases can be completed in any order. Completed phases display a checkmark on the hub card. You can return to the hub between phases at any time.

    <Tip>
      If you need a break, return to the hub and resume a different phase. Your progress within completed phases is saved for the session.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Answer the questions">
    Questions are displayed one at a time with animated transitions. For each question, select the option that best describes the child's typical behaviour:

    | Option | Value | Meaning |
    |--------|-------|---------|
    | **Siempre** | 3 | Performed habitually and independently |
    | **A veces** | 2 | Inconsistent, with assistance, or frequent errors |
    | **Nunca** | 1 | Not performed or not yet acquired |
    | **No sabe / No oportunidad** | 0 | Not observed or no opportunity has arisen |

    A progress bar at the top of the screen shows your completion percentage within the current phase.

    **Navigation:**
    - Use the **back** button to revisit and change a previous answer at any time within the phase.
    - Selecting an answer automatically advances to the next question.

    <Info>
      A small number of questions are **inverted** — for these items, answering "Siempre" (always) indicates a concern rather than a strength. The platform handles this automatically; you do not need to adjust your answers. See [Scoring](/guides/scoring#inverted-questions) for details.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete all four phases">
    After finishing a phase, you are returned to the test hub. Repeat the process for the remaining three phases. All four phases must be completed before you can submit.

    The hub shows a checkmark on each completed phase card so you can track progress at a glance.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit and view results">
    Once all phases are complete, the **Submit** button becomes active. After submitting:

    - Your **total score** is displayed as a fraction of the maximum possible score
    - A **per-phase breakdown** shows scores for Alimentación, Aseo, Vestido, and Funcionamiento General
    - Each phase score is colour-coded according to the risk classification level

    See [Scoring](/guides/scoring) for a full explanation of how scores are calculated and what each level means.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I pause and continue later?">
    The test runs within a single browser session using session storage. If you close the browser tab or window, your progress will be lost. Complete the screening in one sitting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I am unsure how to answer a question?">
    Use **No sabe / No oportunidad** (0) for any behaviour you have not observed or for situations that have not arisen. Do not guess.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I change my answers?">
    Yes. Within a phase, use the back button to return to any previous question and change your answer before completing the phase.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does the order I complete phases matter?">
    No. Phases are independent and can be completed in any order. Each phase score is calculated separately before being combined into a total.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
